## The Strategic Objective

Ireland's EU Council Presidency runs from July to December 2026. This is not a ceremonial role. Ireland will set agendas, chair working groups, and broker compromises on the EU's defence and security architecture at the moment Europe is rewriting it.

Security Ireland's thesis is that the Presidency represents a six-month window to establish Ireland as a permanent, credible contributor to European security — in the domains where its geography and economic position are irreplaceable. After December 2026, the frameworks are set and the political attention moves elsewhere.

## The Three Domains

Ireland cannot compete with France on fighter jets or Germany on tanks. It does not need to. Ireland occupies three positions no other EU member state can replicate:

**Maritime Surveillance.** 880,000 km² of Atlantic EEZ covering critical shipping lanes. Nine naval vessels. No primary radar. No subsea monitoring. The largest unmonitored maritime domain in Europe — and the one through which Atlantic shipping and submarine traffic passes.

**Subsea Infrastructure.** Over 20 major subsea cables make landfall on Irish shores, carrying the vast majority of transatlantic data traffic. The Celtic Interconnector to France arrives by 2027. The Far North Fiber linking Ireland to Alaska, Japan, and Norway is planned for 2026.

**Cyber Resilience.** Nine of ten top US tech firms have their EU headquarters in Ireland. A successful cyber attack on Irish-hosted systems affects data integrity across the entire Union. The 2021 HSE ransomware attack demonstrated the vulnerability.

## Four EU Instruments, One Strategy

Each of the four briefs in this series targets a different EU instrument being shaped during Ireland's term. Together they form a single integrated strategy:

1. **ReArm Europe** — ensures EU funding rules (SAFE, EDIP, MFF) recognise domain-specific contribution, not just military procurement.
2. **PESCO Strategic Review** — ensures the revised binding commitments and assessment framework formalise Ireland's domain-focused model.
3. **Maritime Surveillance Cooperation** — ensures Ireland leads the Atlantic basin response through CISE, EUMSS, and the Cable Action Plan.
4. **Hybrid Threats & Cyber Defence** — ensures Ireland's tech-sector concentration is recognised as a structural contribution to EU cyber resilience.

Remove any one and the others lose coherence. The briefs are a set.

## Three Categories of Dossier

Not every dossier requires the same approach. The Presidency Desk series categorises them into three lanes:

**Ireland leads.** Dossiers where Ireland's geographic or economic position gives it irreplaceable standing. Maritime surveillance, subsea cable security, and cyber resilience fall here. Ireland should not merely chair these discussions — it should propose text, table amendments, and build coalitions.

**Ireland shapes.** Dossiers where the Presidency's procedural authority can be deployed to influence outcomes. PESCO binding commitments, EDIP implementing rules, and MFF defence chapter framing fall here. The chair controls the agenda, the timeline, and the compromise text.

**Ireland facilitates.** Dossiers where Ireland has no distinctive national interest but must chair competently. General defence spending targets, third-country participation frameworks, and EU-NATO cooperation fall here. Competent chairing builds credibility that supports the first two lanes.

## The Cost of Passive Chairing

If Ireland chairs these meetings passively — facilitating discussion without shaping rules — the following outcomes are likely:

SAFE rules remain focused on major weapons platforms. EDIP defines participation as defence manufacturing, excluding Ireland's technology sector. The MFF defence chapter is written generically. The NCSC remains isolated from EU crisis mechanisms. Ireland's 880,000 km² remains the largest unmonitored EEZ in Europe. The free-rider accusation survives the Presidency.

The rules being written during Ireland's term will govern EU defence cooperation for the rest of the decade. They are not revised annually. The Presidency is the window — not a window.
