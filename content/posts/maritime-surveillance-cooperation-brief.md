## Ireland's Maritime Position

Ireland's exclusive economic zone extends to 880,000 km² of Atlantic waters — one of the largest maritime domains in Europe. This territory covers critical shipping lanes, submarine transit routes, and the western terminus of the transatlantic subsea cable network.

Despite this strategic position, Ireland's ability to monitor its own waters remains severely limited. Nine naval vessels provide intermittent surface coverage. There is no primary radar, no persistent subsea monitoring, and no integrated maritime domain awareness system. Ireland is, effectively, the largest blind spot in European maritime surveillance.

## Three Frameworks in Play

Three EU maritime security frameworks are being shaped during Ireland's Presidency:

**CISE (Common Information Sharing Environment).** Ireland joined CISE in April 2025, gaining access to EU-wide maritime surveillance data. But CISE participation is two-way: Ireland must contribute as well as consume. The Presidency period is Ireland's opportunity to demonstrate that it can be a net contributor to Atlantic maritime awareness.

**EUMSS (EU Maritime Security Strategy).** The EUMSS progress report falls due during the Presidency. Ireland will chair the discussion on implementation gaps and priorities. This is the mechanism through which Ireland can formally position its Atlantic EEZ as a priority area for EU maritime surveillance investment.

**EU Cable Action Plan.** The European Commission's Cable Action Plan commits €1 billion for submarine cable security, including the designation of Cable Projects of European Interest. Over 20 major subsea cables make landfall on Irish shores, carrying the vast majority of transatlantic data traffic. Ireland should be leading the Atlantic cable security response — not watching from the margins.

## What Ireland Should Secure

Ireland's objective is to establish itself as the lead nation for Atlantic basin maritime surveillance within EU frameworks:

1. **CISE contribution node.** Ireland should establish a national maritime surveillance fusion centre that contributes Atlantic domain awareness to CISE, not merely consumes data from Mediterranean and Baltic partners.

2. **EUMSS Atlantic priority.** The progress report should identify the Atlantic basin — and Ireland's EEZ specifically — as a priority area for surveillance investment, given the critical infrastructure (cables, shipping lanes) transiting Irish waters.

3. **Cable Projects of European Interest.** Ireland should ensure that transatlantic cable landing sites on Irish shores are designated as Cable Projects of European Interest, qualifying for EU security investment under the Cable Action Plan.

## The Geography Argument

Ireland's maritime surveillance contribution is not optional for Europe. Atlantic shipping, submarine activity, and data traffic transit Irish waters whether Ireland monitors them or not. The question is whether Ireland provides this surveillance as a recognised European contribution — or whether other member states must cover Ireland's blind spot themselves.

The Presidency is the moment to formalise the first option.
