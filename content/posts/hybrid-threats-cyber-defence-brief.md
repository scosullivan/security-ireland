## The Hybrid Threat Landscape

On 16 March 2026, the Council of the European Union approved conclusions on countering hybrid threats. These conclusions set the framework for EU-wide response to election interference, disinformation campaigns, critical infrastructure sabotage, and coordinated cyber attacks. Ireland will chair implementation of these conclusions from July 2026.

This is not abstract. Ireland experienced the consequences of hybrid vulnerability directly in May 2021, when the HSE ransomware attack shut down the national health service's IT systems for months. The attack demonstrated that Ireland's critical infrastructure is vulnerable — and that the consequences extend well beyond Irish borders.

## Ireland's Structural Importance

Nine of ten top US technology companies have their European headquarters in Ireland. This concentration means that a successful cyber attack on Irish-hosted systems affects data integrity, service availability, and regulatory compliance across the entire European Union.

Ireland is not merely a participant in European cyber resilience — it is a structural node. EU cyber policy that does not account for Ireland's tech-sector concentration is incomplete by design.

## Four Frameworks Being Shaped

Four EU frameworks for hybrid threat and cyber defence are under active development during Ireland's Presidency:

**EU Hybrid Toolbox.** The comprehensive framework for coordinated EU response to hybrid threats, covering attribution, sanctions, diplomatic response, and operational coordination. Ireland will chair the next iteration of the toolbox's implementation guidelines.

**Cyber Diplomacy Toolbox.** The EU's framework for responding to malicious cyber activities through diplomatic measures, including sanctions and public attribution. Ireland chairs discussions on threshold criteria and response coordination.

**Cybersecurity Crisis Blueprint (EU CyCLONe).** The operational framework for large-scale cybersecurity incident management. Ireland's NCSC should be integrated into EU crisis response mechanisms — currently, it operates in relative isolation.

**NIS2 Implementation.** The revised Network and Information Security Directive requires member state implementation by October 2024. Compliance is uneven. Ireland chairs discussions on enforcement, cross-border incident reporting, and sectoral implementation — with particular relevance given the concentration of regulated entities hosted in Ireland.

## What Ireland Should Secure

1. **NCSC integration.** Ireland's National Cyber Security Centre should be formally integrated into EU crisis response mechanisms (CyCLONe), with dedicated liaison capacity and real-time information sharing protocols.

2. **Tech-sector recognition.** EU hybrid threat and cyber defence frameworks should explicitly recognise hosting concentration as a form of systemic importance — and therefore a qualifying contribution to EU security, not merely a vulnerability to be managed.

3. **Presidency cybersecurity exercise.** Ireland should host a Presidency-level cybersecurity crisis exercise in October 2026, demonstrating operational capacity and building institutional relationships that outlast the six-month term.

4. **NIS2 enforcement coordination.** As the host state for most major US tech firms' EU operations, Ireland should chair NIS2 enforcement coordination in a way that demonstrates regulatory seriousness — building credibility that supports broader claims of cyber contribution.

## The Institutional Gap

The core challenge is that Ireland's systemic importance in EU cyber resilience is not matched by its institutional capacity. The NCSC remains under-resourced compared to peer agencies. Intelligence architecture is fragmented. Cross-departmental coordination on hybrid threats lacks the formal structures common in other EU member states.

The Presidency is an opportunity to close this gap — not by solving every institutional problem in six months, but by locking Ireland into EU frameworks that create ongoing obligations, funding eligibility, and partnership structures that persist beyond December 2026.
