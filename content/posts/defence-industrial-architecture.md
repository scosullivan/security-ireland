## The argument

Ireland has approximately 550 dual-use technology firms, a €2.4 billion dual-use export market, and a technology talent pool shaped by hosting the European headquarters of nine of the ten largest US technology companies. It sits at the intersection of three domains — maritime, subsea, and cyber — where EU funding instruments are allocating tens of billions.

And yet Irish firms cannot participate in classified EU defence programmes because there is no security clearance system. They cannot win supply chain positions on domestic procurement because there is no industrial participation policy. They cannot independently export defence technology because there is no export control framework.

## What this paper provides

This paper maps the full architecture gap between Ireland's technology capacity and its institutional readiness for defence production. It defines Ireland's bounded role within European defence: not an independent arms industry, but a domain-specific contributor through EU frameworks in maritime AI, cybersecurity, subsea monitoring, and dual-use sensing.
