## The Strategic Review

The Permanent Structured Cooperation (PESCO) is due its third strategic review in late 2026 — during Ireland's EU Council Presidency. Ireland will chair the discussions that determine how PESCO's binding commitments are rewritten, how compliance is assessed, and how PESCO participation connects to the new generation of EU defence funding instruments.

This is not a routine administrative exercise. The review will shape the institutional framework for European defence cooperation for the remainder of the decade.

## Ireland's PESCO Portfolio

Ireland participates in 7 of 68 active PESCO projects. All seven are concentrated in domains that align with Ireland's strategic thesis: maritime surveillance, subsea infrastructure, cyber resilience, and military logistics.

This concentration is deliberate. Ireland's PESCO portfolio represents the institutional evidence of its contribution to European security in the domains where its geography is irreplaceable. The strategic review will determine whether this domain-focused model is recognised as a coherent strategy — or assessed as insufficient breadth of participation.

## The PESCO-Funding Connection

PESCO participation is increasingly becoming a gateway to EU defence funding. The EDIP programme gives preferential terms to projects with PESCO alignment. The SAFE facility is expected to follow suit. The MFF defence chapter will reference PESCO commitments as eligibility criteria.

This means the strategic review's outcome directly affects Ireland's access to European defence funding. If the revised framework rewards broad participation across many capability areas, Ireland's focused portfolio is disadvantaged. If it recognises domain excellence as a valid model, Ireland's contribution is institutionally validated.

## What Ireland Should Secure

Ireland should seek three outcomes from the strategic review:

1. **Domain excellence pathway.** A revised assessment mechanism that recognises concentrated, high-quality participation in specific domains as equivalent to broader but shallower participation across many areas.

2. **PESCO-EDIP-SAFE alignment.** Explicit language connecting PESCO project participation to eligibility for EU defence funding instruments, with domain-specific contribution qualifying alongside traditional military capability.

3. **Project consolidation around delivery.** The 68-project portfolio includes many that have delivered little. Ireland should chair consolidation discussions that concentrate resources on projects with demonstrated capability delivery — including Ireland's own maritime and cyber projects.

## Ukraine Dimension

Ukraine's participation in PESCO projects is under active discussion. Ireland should support Ukraine's inclusion in maritime surveillance and cyber defence projects specifically, creating a coalition of interest that strengthens both Ukraine's contribution and Ireland's domain leadership.
