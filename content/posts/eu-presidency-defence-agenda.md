## Overview

Ireland's forthcoming presidency of the Council of the European Union presents a unique opportunity to shape the EU's defence agenda during a period of significant strategic transition.

## Key Priorities

### European Strategic Autonomy

Supporting the development of European capabilities independent of external dependencies remains central to Irish presidency objectives.

### Defence Industrial Cooperation

Strengthening European defence industrial base through cooperative programmes and capability development.

### Burden-Sharing Framework

Advancing discussions on equitable distribution of defence responsibilities and capabilities across member states.

## Implementation Mechanisms

The presidency will work through multiple channels to advance these objectives:

- Council working groups and ministerial meetings
- Engagement with European Commission and High Representative
- Bilateral consultation with member states
- Strategic coordination with NATO allies

## Conclusion

A successful presidency in defence matters requires balancing Ireland's traditional perspectives with contemporary security realities.
