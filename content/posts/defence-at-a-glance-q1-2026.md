## Defence Budget and Spending

Ireland's total defence budget for 2026 stands at €1.24 billion, representing a year-on-year increase of 6.2% compared to 2025. Personnel costs remain the dominant share at 58%, reflecting the labour-intensive nature of defence operations and ongoing recruitment and retention efforts under the Commission on the Defence Forces recommendations.

<img src="/images/charts/budget_donut.png" alt="Budget Allocation 2026 — Personnel 58%, Equipment 28%, Operations 14%" width="400" />

**Budget Allocation by Category, 2026**

| Category | Amount | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Personnel costs | €719m | 58% |
| Equipment &amp; maintenance | €347m | 28% |
| Operations &amp; infrastructure | €174m | 14% |
| **Total** | **€1,240m** | **100%** |

*Source: Department of Defence, Revised Estimates 2026.*

## Personnel Strength

The Irish Defence Forces stood at 7,650 personnel as of March 2026, comprising the Permanent Defence Force. An additional ~2,100 reservists are mobilizable. The Army accounts for the largest share with 5,800 personnel, followed by the Naval Service (1,050) and Air Corps (800).

## Naval and Air Capabilities

<img src="/images/charts/capabilities.png" alt="Naval Service 8 vessels and Air Corps 9 aircraft breakdown" width="430" />

The Naval Service operates 8 active vessels: 2 Eithne-class offshore patrol vessels, 4 Oileán Gheálach-class coastal patrol vessels, and 2 inshore patrol craft. Extended maritime surveillance and offshore patrol capacity remain key capability gaps, with a vessel replacement programme pending.

The Air Corps maintains 9 aircraft in service: 5 Cessna 206 light aircraft for patrol, 2 Sikorsky S-61 helicopters for transport and search-and-rescue, and 2 BN2 Islander light aircraft for patrol. A procurement process for new medium-lift helicopter capability is ongoing.

## International Operations and Commitments

Ireland had 625 personnel deployed overseas in Q1 2026 across United Nations missions (425), EU missions (125), and other bilateral operations (75). The largest single deployment is UNIFIL in Lebanon with 250 personnel.

## PESCO Participation

Ireland participates in 12 active PESCO projects as of March 2026, spanning three key domains: military mobility and training (5 projects), maritime security (4 projects), and cyber defence (3 projects).

## Defence Spending Trends

Ireland's defence spending has increased steadily in recent years, reflecting recognition of evolving security challenges. However, at approximately 0.45% of GDP, Irish defence spending remains well below the EU average for non-allied states and below many comparable European nations.
