## Defence Budget and Spending

**Total Defence Budget 2026:** €1.24 billion
- Personnel costs: ~58% (€719 million)
- Equipment and maintenance: ~28% (€347 million)
- Operations and infrastructure: ~14% (€174 million)

**Year-on-Year Growth:** +6.2% compared to 2025

## Personnel Strength

**Irish Defence Forces Total Strength:** 7,650 personnel (as of March 2026)
- Permanent Defence Force: 7,650
- Reserve force: ~2,100 (mobilizable)

**Breakdown by Service:**
- Army: 4,800
- Naval Service: 1,100
- Air Corps: 900
- Defence Forces administration: 850

## Naval Capabilities

**Active Vessels:** 8
- 2 Eithne-class offshore patrol vessels
- 4 Oileán Gheálach-class coastal patrol vessels
- 2 inshore patrol craft

**Key Capability Gaps:** Extended maritime surveillance and offshore patrol capacity

## Air Capabilities

**Aircraft in Service:** 9
- 5 Cessna 206 light aircraft (patrol)
- 2 Sikorsky S-61 helicopters (transport/SAR)
- 2 BN2 Islander light aircraft (patrol)

**Recent Developments:** Procurement process for new medium-lift helicopter capability ongoing

## International Operations and Commitments

**Personnel Deployed (Q1 2026):** 625
- United Nations missions: 425
- EU missions: 125
- Other bilateral operations: 75

**Key Missions:**
- UNIFIL (Lebanon): 250
- UNISFA (Abyei): 80
- EUFOR (Central African Republic): 75
- EU Training Missions: 50

## PESCO Participation

**Active Projects:** 12 (as of March 2026)
- Maritime security: 4 projects
- Cyber defence: 3 projects
- Military mobility and training: 5 projects

## Defence Spending Trends

Ireland's defence spending has increased steadily in recent years, reflecting recognition of evolving security challenges. However, at approximately 0.45% of GDP, Irish defence spending remains below NATO's 2% benchmark and below many comparable European nations.
