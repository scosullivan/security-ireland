## The problem

Ireland has approximately 550 dual-use technology firms, a €2.4 billion dual-use export market, and an emerging €2.5 billion domestic defence procurement pipeline. But three institutional gaps prevent these assets from becoming a defence industrial base.

There is no security clearance system — so Irish firms cannot access classified EU programmes. There is no industrial participation policy — so domestic procurement does not generate supply chain positions for Irish firms. There is no export control framework aligned with international regimes — so Irish firms cannot independently export defence technology.

## What this paper provides

This is the operational companion to the Defence Industrial Architecture paper. Where that paper diagnoses the architecture gap, this paper provides the step-by-step blueprints for building each system: the institutional home, the legislation, the staffing, the IT infrastructure, and the interaction with EU and NATO frameworks.
