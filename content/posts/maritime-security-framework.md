## Introduction

Ireland's maritime domain increasingly shapes our national security landscape. With over 900 kilometres of coastline and extensive exclusive economic zones, the maritime domain represents both opportunity and vulnerability in contemporary security planning.

## Strategic Context

The strategic environment surrounding Irish maritime interests has undergone significant transformation in recent years. Heightened Russian naval activity in European waters, evolving patterns of maritime commerce disruption, and climate-driven changes to sea routes all demand reassessment of Ireland's maritime posture.

### Key Challenges

- Naval capabilities monitoring
- Fisheries protection and enforcement
- Search and rescue coordination
- Environmental protection in maritime zones

## Current Framework

Ireland's current maritime security approach operates through multiple institutional channels, including the Irish Defence Forces Naval Service, the Irish Coast Guard, and various international maritime bodies.

### Institutional Actors

The Naval Service operates three offshore patrol vessels and maintains a significant portion of Irish Defence Forces personnel. The Coast Guard coordinates rescue operations and maritime law enforcement activities.

## Policy Recommendations

This paper recommends a comprehensive review of Ireland's maritime security strategy, with particular attention to:

1. **Equipment modernisation** - Current platforms are aging and increasingly inadequate for contemporary challenges
2. **Personnel development** - Enhanced training and career pathways for maritime professionals
3. **International cooperation** - Deepened engagement with EU maritime security initiatives
4. **Technology integration** - Advanced surveillance and monitoring capabilities

## Conclusion

Ireland's maritime security framework must evolve to meet contemporary challenges while maintaining the nation's traditional commitment to international law and peaceful resolution of disputes.
